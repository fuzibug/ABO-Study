Domain I: General Ophthalmic Knowledge (25%)

31 Questions on Exam

A. Refractive Conditions

  • Emmetropia: Normal vision – light focuses directly on retina.
  • Myopia (Nearsightedness): Light focuses in front of retina, corrected with minus (–) lenses.
  • Hyperopia (Farsightedness): Light focuses behind retina, corrected with plus (+) lenses.
  • Presbyopia: Age-related loss of accommodation (typically 40+); requires near add.
  • Astigmatism: Irregular corneal curvature causing blur at all distances; needs cylinder correction.
  • Anisometropia: Significant difference in prescription between the two eyes.

B. Eye Anatomy

  • Cornea: Clear front surface, provides ~2/3 of eye's focusing power (~+43D).
  • Lens: Changes shape for accommodation, provides ~1/3 of focusing power (~+20D).
  • Retina: Light-sensitive layer containing rods and cones.
  • Macula / Fovea: Central retina for sharp central vision.
  • Optic Nerve: Transmits visual information to brain.

C. Common Ocular Pathologies

  • Cataract: Opacification of crystalline lens causing glare and decreased acuity.
  • Glaucoma: Often associated with elevated IOP, leads to optic nerve damage.
  • Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD): Central vision loss (dry and wet forms).
  • Diabetic Retinopathy: Retinal vascular changes due to diabetes.
  • Keratoconus: Progressive corneal thinning producing irregular astigmatism.

Domain II: Ophthalmic Optics (25%)

31 Questions on Exam

Essential Formulas

Prentice's Rule
Δ = F × d
Δ = prism diopters, F = lens power (D), d = decentration (cm).
Lens Power Formula
D = 1/f (meters)
D = 100/f (centimeters)
Spherical Equivalent
SE = Sphere + (Cylinder ÷ 2)

Reading Prescriptions

  • Spherical Equivalent: SE = sphere + (cylinder ÷ 2). Useful for comparing prescriptions.
  • Transposition: Add sphere + cyl (new sphere), change cyl sign, add/subtract 90° to axis.
  • Example: –2.00 –1.50 × 090 → –3.50 +1.50 × 180.

Prism Fundamentals

  • Prism Diopter (Δ): 1Δ = 1 cm deviation at 1 m.
  • Minus Lens: Base direction opposite decentration.
  • Plus Lens: Base direction same as decentration.

Lens Materials

  • CR-39 (n=1.498): Standard plastic, good optics, affordable.
  • Polycarbonate (n=1.586): Impact resistant, thin, built-in UV.
  • Trivex (n=1.532): Impact resistant, light, higher Abbe than poly.
  • High-Index 1.60: ~20% thinner than CR-39.
  • High-Index 1.67: ~30% thinner than CR-39.
  • High-Index 1.74: ~35% thinner than CR-39; thinnest available.

Domain III: Dispensing Procedures (20%)

25 Questions on Exam

Key Measurements

  • PD (Pupillary Distance): Average adult 62–64 mm.
  • Monocular PD: Center of bridge to each pupil.
  • Seg Height: Bottom of lens to top of segment.
  • Vertex Distance: Standard 12–14 mm.
  • Pantoscopic Tilt: 8–12° typical.

Dispensing Scenarios

  • High Minus: Small eye size, high-index lens, minimal wrap.
  • Progressive Non-Adapt: Recheck PD/height, verify panto/wrap.
  • Computer User: Occupational lens, AR + blue filter.
  • Safety: ANSI Z87.1, poly/Trivex, side shields.

Domain IV: Ophthalmic Products (20%)

25 Questions on Exam

Lens Types

  • Single Vision: One prescription throughout.
  • Bifocal: Distance (top) + near (segment).
  • Trifocal: Distance, intermediate, near.
  • Progressive: Gradual power change, no line.

Coatings & Treatments

  • AR: Reduces reflections, improves clarity.
  • UV Protection: Blocks UV-A (315–380 nm) and UV-B (280–315 nm).
  • Blue Light: Reduces HEV (380–500 nm).
  • Photochromic: Darkens in UV.
  • Polarized: Eliminates horizontal glare.

Laws & Standards

  • ANSI Z80.1: Tolerances for finished eyewear.
  • Duty to Warn: Inform about impact resistance.
  • Lensmeter: Verify prescription, prism, OC, add.
